Three small poems, for you

10 Years

10 years ago I met you in a crowded, ordinary hall, and somehow, out of all those faces, yours was the one that felt like quiet. You were kind before kindness was easy, gentle when the world gave no reason to be — and through every season that tried to move us, you stayed. You always stayed. If I have done one thing right in ten years, it was noticing, early, that you were rare — and never, for a single day, letting myself forget it.

The Way You Love People

You read people the way others read the weather — you feel the storm in someone before they speak, and you stay, umbrella in hand, asking nothing back. You put yourself last so gently that no one even notices the giving — but I notice, Khairat. I have always noticed. The world is softer because you move through it. I am softer because you stayed near me.

A Wish, On Your Day

On the day the world received you, it grew kinder without knowing why. So today I wish you back every good thing you've ever given away — a year that holds you the way you hold everyone, that listens when you're tired of being strong, that hands you the same patience and grace you've spent ten years spending on me. May goodness find you the way you find everyone else — first, and without being asked.

The last page

Khairat,

I've been trying to find the right words for ten years, so forgive me if these still aren't perfect.

You are the kindest person I have ever known — not in the loud way people perform kindness, but in the quiet, steady way that asks for nothing back. You feel what people feel. You think of everyone before yourself. You were doing it as a kid in those school corridors, and you're still doing it now.

Somewhere in those ten years, you became the closest person to me. The one I trust. The one I think of first. The one who made me believe that gentleness is the strongest thing a person can be.

I'm not sure I've ever told you properly how much you mean to me — so let today be the day I do. You matter to me more than I know how to say. I care about you, deeply. And of all the people in that crowded hall ten years ago, I am so grateful it was you.

Happy birthday, Khairat. Thank you for being you. The world is better for it. I am better for it.
